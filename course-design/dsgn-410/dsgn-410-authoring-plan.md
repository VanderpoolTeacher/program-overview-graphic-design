## Open questions

These need resolution before authoring proceeds:

1. **Credit-hour budget.** 1 credit × 16 weeks ≈ 48 hours of student
   work. Formative weeks (1–9) ~22 hours + Summative (10–16)
   ~16–20 hours = 38–42 hours. Fits at the upper end of 1 credit
   but tight. If course is offered as 2 or 3 credits, scope can
   expand. Decision needed.

2. **DSGN 491 Internship coordination.** Several formatives and the
   summative anchor in the concurrent internship. Requires advance
   coordination with the internship coordinator to ensure DSGN 491
   is genuinely concurrent (not staggered).

3. **Anonymized real contracts.** Week 6 walks through a real
   (anonymized) freelance contract. Source needed: instructor's own,
   guest practitioner's, or a pre-cleared sample. Cannot use
   off-the-shelf templates for this — must be a real contract that
   shows real decisions.

4. **Guest practitioners (Week 16).** Final pitch day benefits from
   an external practitioner reviewer. Cultivating that network is
   ongoing program work.

5. **AI-tool currency.** The AI landscape shifts every 6–12 months.
   Need a plan for keeping Week 5 (rate research) and Week 9 (AI
   workflow) materials current — not assignment-by-assignment, but
   institutionally.

6. **Multi-instructor calibration.** If DSGN 410 is taught by
   different faculty in future cohorts, how is the rubric (especially
   the new criterion 7 "adaptive revision under constraint")
   calibrated? Sample grading session built into faculty
   development?

## Authoring sequence

Suggested order to author the formative assignments (the summative
is already done at `assignments/01-practice-launch-summative.md`):

1. **#3 Practice statement formative (Week 3)** — direct precursor
   to summative Part A; author first to anchor the rest.
2. **#5 Proposal + budget + timeline (Week 5)** — biggest formative;
   author second so other artifacts can reference its fictional
   brief.
3. **#6 Contract excerpt + #7 Sample invoice (Weeks 6, 7)** — author
   together since both come from splitting the existing Legal and
   Financial Responsibilities draft.
4. **#4 Mock intake email (Week 4)** — author after #5 so the intake
   email matches the proposal's project.
5. **#8 Ethics scenario response (Week 8)** — needs the practice
   statement to reference; produces raw material for both summative
   reflection AND Event 2.
6. **#9 Pitch sketch + AI workflow (Week 9)** — author last among
   formatives; needs all prior content to reference.
7. **#1 Practice models reflection + #2 Career profile + #0
   Onboarding (Weeks 1, 2)** — author last; lowest-stakes,
   formula-light.
