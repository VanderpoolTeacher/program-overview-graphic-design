## What the source actually says

**Purpose** (verbatim from `source/program-data.md`):

> Preparing students for the freelance, agency, and entrepreneurial side of
> the industry — pricing, contracts, intellectual property, project management
> and client relations, business models (freelance, agency, in-house),
> marketing and self-promotion, invoicing/proposals/legal basics, ethics and
> professional standards, and creative entrepreneurship.

That is the entirety of the course-design guidance in the source. The
document describes **what** the course covers, never **how**, **in what
sequence**, **with what tools**, or **measured against what rubrics**.

## Patterns worth noting

1. **MLO 8 (Entrepreneurship) is the through-line.** Every CLO maps to
   MLO 8. This is *the* entrepreneurship course. If a CLO doesn't make a
   student more business-ready, it doesn't belong here.

2. **MLO 5 (Data Visualization) appears only at CLO 5** — the lone data-viz
   hook in a business course. New course content should make sure CLO 5
   actually requires a data-driven artifact, not just a generic presentation.

3. **AI framing differs from every other course.** In DSGN 105 / 107 / 220 /
   etc., AI is a *creative* augmentation tool. In DSGN 410, AI is an
   *operations* tool — estimating, drafting, client comms, market research.
   Course content should pick AI tools accordingly (e.g., contract-clause
   suggesters, market-research helpers — not image generators).

4. **One credit / six CLOs is a tight ratio.** Implies a survey course:
   broad coverage, shallow execution. Don't design as if there are 3
   credits' worth of room.

5. **Positioned alongside DSGN 491 (Internship).** A senior-spring student
   is simultaneously running a real-world internship and taking this course.
   Design assignments to leverage the internship — e.g., "draft a contract
   for a project you actually did at your internship" — instead of
   ignoring it.
